    \textbf{Abstract}

    The telecommunications industry faces challenges in predicting the revenue of international SMS services, which have unique characteristics and are influenced by external factors. Although machine learning approaches have been widely used for revenue prediction, studies exploring the use of Genetic Algorithm as a hyperparameter optimization technique in machine learning models, particularly in the context of international SMS service revenue prediction, remain relatively limited. This research implements Genetic Algorithm for hyperparameter optimization on machine learning models, specifically tree-based models, to address the problem of predicting international SMS service revenue at Company X. The primary dataset used is derived from international SMS traffic data of Company X covering the period of 2023 to 2024. Experiments were conducted by comparing hyperparameter optimization using Genetic Algorithm against baseline models without optimization on tree-based machine learning models. The results demonstrate that the application of Genetic Algorithm for hyperparameter optimization is capable of improving the performance of tree-based models in predicting revenue, achieving a WMAPE of 16.29\% for XGBoost + GA and 20.97\% for Random Forest + GA, compared to the baseline WMAPE of 25.37\% for Random Forest and 27.12\% for XGBoost.
